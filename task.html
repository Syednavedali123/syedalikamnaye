Early Life

Ali Khamenei was born on 19 April 1939 in Mashhad, a famous religious city in Iran. He was born into a religious and modest family. His father, Javad Khamenei, was a respected Islamic scholar who taught religious knowledge. Because of this environment, Ali Khamenei grew up learning about Islam, morality, and discipline from a very young age. His family lived a simple life, and he experienced the struggles of ordinary people. From childhood he showed interest in reading and learning. He studied the Quran, Arabic language, and Islamic law in local religious schools. Besides religious studies, he also liked literature and poetry. These interests helped him develop strong thinking and communication skills. The environment of Mashhad, which is an important religious center, played an important role in shaping his personality and beliefs. Role in the Iranian Revolution

During the 1960s and 1970s, Iran was ruled by the Shah, and many religious leaders and scholars opposed his government. Ali Khamenei joined the movement that criticized the Shah’s policies and supported Islamic governance. Because of his political activities and speeches, he was arrested several times and faced imprisonment. Khamenei supported the movement led by Ruhollah Khomeini, which later became the Iranian Revolution. This revolution overthrew the monarchy and replaced it with the Islamic Republic of Iran in 1979. After the revolution, Khamenei became one of the important figures helping to establish the new political and religious system. His role in spreading revolutionary ideas and organizing supporters increased his influence in Iranian politics.

Political Career and Presidency

After the revolution, Khamenei began serving in several important government positions. He became a member of Iran’s parliament and later worked in key revolutionary institutions. His leadership abilities and loyalty to the new government helped him gain the trust of other political leaders. During the Iran–Iraq War, Iran faced serious challenges. Khamenei played an active role in supporting the country during this difficult time. In 1981, he was elected as the President of Iran. He served as president for two terms from 1981 to 1989. As president, he focused on strengthening the country’s political stability, supporting the war effort, and improving the government system.

Supreme Leader of Iran

In 1989, after the death of Ruhollah Khomeini, Iran needed a new Supreme Leader to guide the country. The Assembly of Experts selected Ali Khamenei as the next Supreme Leader. This position is the highest authority in Iran and has significant power over the country’s political, military, and religious decisions. As Supreme Leader, Khamenei supervises the armed forces, judiciary, and important state institutions. He also plays a major role in shaping Iran’s domestic and foreign policies. Over the years, he has influenced many political decisions and guided the country through different economic and political challenges. Today he remains one of the most powerful and influential leaders in the Middle East.
